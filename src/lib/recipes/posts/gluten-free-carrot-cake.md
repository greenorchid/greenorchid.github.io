# Gluten-Free Carrot Cake with Mascarpone Frosting

A delicious, moist carrot cake, made to my favourite ingredients filled with sweet raisins and crunchy walnuts. An extra lift from baking powder and baking soda helps the gluten-free batter rise tall despite the heavy carrots and nuts. A silky mascarpone frosting adds a lightly tangy finish that's not too sweet. This is indistinguishable from a non-gluten free recipe, and swapping the butter for oil means this keeps moist and lasts a long time (not that that will be a problem with this firm favourite!)

## Instructions

1. **Preheat oven** to 185°C. Grease and line a 20 cm round or square cake tin.

2. **Hydrate raisins:** Cover raisins with hot water and soak for 5–10 minutes. Drain thoroughly.

3. **Salt the walnuts (optional bakery trick):** Toss chopped walnuts with a tiny pinch of salt.

4. **Mix wet ingredients:** In a large bowl whisk oil, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth.

5. Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition.

6. Stir in vanilla, buttermilk, and apple juice (if using).

7. **Combine dry ingredients:** Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and ginger.

8. Fold dry ingredients into the wet mixture until just combined.

9. Fold in grated carrots, raisins, and walnuts.

10. **Rest the batter for 10 minutes** to hydrate the gluten-free flour.

11. Pour batter into the tin (it should fill about ¾ of the tin).

12. Bake **10 minutes at 185°C**, then reduce oven to **175°C** and bake another **40–50 minutes** until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean.

13. Cool completely before frosting.

## Frosting

1. Beat mascarpone, cing sugar and vanilla. until smooth
2. Spread evenly over the cooled cake.
3. Decorate with walnuts sprinkled on top

## Tips

- Resting the batter improves gluten-free texture and prevents grit.
- Soaking raisins keeps them plump and prevents them stealing moisture from the cake.
- Toasting walnuts lightly deepens flavour.
- This cake tastes even better the next day once the spices settle.
