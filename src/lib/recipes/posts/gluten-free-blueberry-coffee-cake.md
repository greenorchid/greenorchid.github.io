# Gluten-Free Blueberry Coffee Cake

An adapted recipe, tweaked to be gluten free, from a recipe I discovered online. Using buttermil, it results in a tender, cinnamon-swirled coffee cake bursting with fresh blueberries. I preferred the toasted hazelnutz and oats to top within the crumb. This version is optimised specifically for gluten-free structure using Odlums Gluten Free Self-Raising Flour, ensuring a soft crumb and balanced rise.

1. **Preheat oven** to 175°C. Grease and line a 20 cm (8x8") metal tin.
2. **Prepare crumb topping:** Rub flour, brown sugar, salt, pecans, and cold butter together until large moist crumbs form. Add oats & mix. Chill.
3. **Mix cinnamon layer:** Combine sugar and cinnamon. Set aside.
4. **Prepare blueberries:** Wash & Thoroughly dry blueberries
5. **Cream butter and sugar** for 3–4 minutes until pale and fluffy.
6. Beat in vanilla, then eggs one at a time.
7. Whisk flour, baking soda, salt, and xanthan gum (if using).
8. Add dry ingredients and buttermilk alternately to the butter mixture. Mix slightly longer than a standard cake (about 30 seconds extra) to hydrate the gluten-free flour.
9. Rest batter for 10 minutes. This is important to hydrate the GF flour blend.
10. Spread half the batter into the tin.
11. Sprinkle cinnamon sugar evenly.
12. Spread remaining batter gently over top.
13. Scatter half the blueberries.
14. Add crumb topping.
15. Finish with remaining blueberries.
16. Bake 60–70 minutes, until golden and a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean. This takes longer than a non-gf bake.
17. Cool in the tin for at least 30 minutes before slicing.

## Tips

- Resting the batter improves gluten-free texture.
- Cool fully before slicing for clean cuts.
- Store airtight at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate up to 5 days.
