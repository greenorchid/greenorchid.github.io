# Breakfast Granola

Created as a challenge to replicate a natural gluten-free granola, and save some Euros too. This quick recipe is a close match, avoids "unnaturally-natural" ingredients (concentrated date juice isn't used), and has poroven to be ~15% of the cost. Approved by the breakfast eaters, and storing easily in airtight containers, this is on the weekly rotation (or more during school breaks!)

## Instructions

1. **Preheat oven** to 150°C. Line a flat try with parchment paper.

2. **Mix dry ingredients:** Mix oats, nuts, cinnamon, ginger & salt together.

3. **Whisk Egg white:** Whisk egg white until frothy.

4. **Mix wet ingredients:** In a large bowl mix maple syrup, melted oil, egg white and vanilla.

5. Combine dry & wet ingredients thorughly.

6. Spread on tray and **press firmly** into a compact layer.

7. Remove from oven, and allow to cool completely in the tray. This will "solidfy" everything together.

8. Once fully cool, break into chunks of desired size and mix in raisins.

9. Store in an airtight container. Will keep for a week or two, but I'm sure you won't have that problem.

## Tips

- Simply swap in gluten fre oats for a GF friendly option (Lidl's "Just Free" brand works great).
- Cluster & nut size are up to you. We like marble-sized chunks in the cereal.
- This is also handy for leftover dried fruits (cranberries, chopped dates etc. all have been used).
- Pretty much any nuts or seeds can be used (Pumpkin seeds & chopped hazlenuts add a nice twist)
- Serve however you see fit, with yoghurt, milk, fresh fruit or just as plain as a alpine-style "trail mix".
