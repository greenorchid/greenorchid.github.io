# No-Bake Toblerone Cheesecake

Originally crafted from leftovers including a desire to use condensed milk instead of sugar, and perfected to be rich, indulgent and slightly less "cheesey", this indulgent no-bake cheesecake combines the iconic honey-almond chocolate flavor of Toblerone with a silky, creamy filling and a buttery biscuit base. Perfect for special occasions or when you want a show-stopping dessert without turning on the oven. A favourite and frequently requested by a brother as a bring-along for dinners!

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the base**  
   Blitz the digestive biscuits into fine crumbs. Mix with the melted butter until fully combined. Press firmly into the base of a lined 20–22 cm springform tin. Chill in the fridge while preparing the filling.

2. **Melt the Toblerone**  
   Place the chopped Toblerone in a heatproof bowl. Melt gently over simmering water or in short microwave bursts, stirring until smooth. Set aside to cool until warm (not hot).

3. **Bloom the gelatin**  
   Soak the gelatin leaves in very cold water for 8–10 minutes until soft and floppy.

4. **Make the cheesecake mixture**  
   Beat the cream cheese and mascarpone together until smooth. Add the condensed milk and salt, mixing well. Stir in the melted Toblerone until fully combined.  
   In a separate bowl, whip 150 ml of the double cream to soft peaks, then gently fold it into the mixture.

5. **Melt & temper the gelatin**  
   Remove gelatin from the water and squeeze out excess liquid.  
   Gently heat 50 ml double cream in a small saucepan over low heat (do not boil). Add gelatin and stir until dissolved.  
   Mix 4–5 tablespoons of the cheesecake mixture into the gelatin mixture, then pour it back into the main bowl while stirring gently.  
   Fold in the remaining chopped Toblerone for texture.

6. **Assemble & chill**  
   Pour the filling over the chilled biscuit base and smooth the top. Refrigerate for at least 6–8 hours, preferably overnight.

7. **Serve**  
   Remove from the fridge 10 minutes before slicing. Use a warm knife for clean cuts. Decorate with Toblerone pieces if desired.

## Tips

- Let the melted chocolate cool slightly before mixing to avoid splitting the filling.
- Tempering the gelatin is essential for a smooth, lump-free texture.
- For cleaner slices, wipe your knife between cuts.
- Store covered in the fridge for up to 3–4 days.
- The metled toblerone includes nougat-thse will remain as small white pieces incorporated in the cheesecake mixture.
